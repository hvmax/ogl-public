### Top 10 Creative Leadership Books of 2020

21 November 2020|Everyday Leadership, Leadership, Leadership Development
Over the last decade, while serving as the Faculty Director of the Berlin School of Creative Leadership, OGL's David Slocum published quarterly summaries of recommended new readings for creative leaders that culminated, each year, with an annual Top 10 list of recommended books. Those titles consistently combined the insights and experiences of practitioners, personal accounts of leaders, and path-setting research from academics and consultancies. As the extraordinary year of 2020 draws to a close,On Global Leadershipis pleased to showcase the latest list of valuable readings for leaders and others that have appeared since January.
This list of the Top 10 Creative Leadership Books of 2020 offers concrete advice and powerful ideas about becoming better and more creative leaders, and they capture some of the evolving priorities that confront nearly all contemporary leaders, workers, and citizens. In fact, despite mostly being written before the combined crises of the Coronavirus pandemic, the economic recession, and social and racial justice, these recommendations admirably address how leadership can navigate the complexity and uncertainty that now seems commonplace.
CREATIVE LEADERSHIP BOOK OF THE YEAR – 2020
Rishad Tobaccawala,Restoring the Soul of Business: Staying Human in the Age of Data(Harper Collins Leadership)
After a storied career in the marketing and communications industries, the former Chief Growth Officer of the Publicis Groupe has written what is at once a manifesto and a guidebook for leaders and organizations striving to integrate digital tools and data-driven insights with analog people and human intuition. His insightful yet practical recommendations cover a range of urgent topics, from how to think about the future and upgrade one’s mental operating system to leading with soul and adapting to change "so it sucks less". A vital read for all those creative leaders wanting to be proactive in driving the business transformations required for success amidst ongoing technological, market, and social changes.
Jeff Bezos,Invent and Wander: The Collected Writings of Jeff Bezos, with an Introduction by Walter Isaacson (PublicAffairs & Harvard Business Review Press)
This collection of Jeff Bezos's writings – his 23 annual shareholder letters, plus numerous speeches and interviews, provides exceptional insight into the evolution of the Amazon founder’s thinking. From the priority of a "Day 1 mindset" and being customer-obsessed to the importance of culture, a willingness to fail, and the linkages between business and public policy, biographer Isaacson, in an excellent introduction, identifies the core tenets held by Bezos, the innovator today whom he claims is in the league of da Vinci, Lovelace, Einstein, and Jobs.
Annie Duke,How to Decide: Simple Rules for Making Better Choices(Portfolio)
The former professional poker player, author ofThinking in Bets, and now Wharton School professor has assembled a valuable toolkit for analyzing and making more effective decisions in business and life. Using stories, exercises, and thought experiments, she covers a host of key topics – from biases, feedback, and luck to fast, slow, and advance decisions – that help leaders to become more confident and better decision-makers.
B. J. Fogg,Tiny Habits: The Small Changes that Change Everything(Mariner Books)
Having the ability to understand and change behaviors, whether others’ or one’s own, is central to the sustainable growth and success of leaders. The founder and director of the Behavior Design Lab at Stanford uses clear explanations and helpful exercises to describe how the elements of behavior (motivation, ability, prompts) enable habit change and then shows how "tiny" changes can both help to grow new habits and untangle bad ones.
Frances Frei and Anne Morriss,Unleashed: The Unapologetic Leader’s Guide to EmpoweringEveryone Around You(Harvard Business Review Press)
The Harvard Business School professor andTED Talk speaker on how to build (and re-build) trustand the Executive Founder of the Leadership Consortium argue that real leadership is about how effective one is at making other people better. Grounded in recent, successful case examples, they have written a practical guide for leaders about how to use trust, love, and inclusion to create a spaces and drive interactions in which others can excel and thrive.
Deborah Gruenfeld,Acting with Power: Why We Are More Powerful Than We Believe(Currency)
For leaders, power is often reduced to a zero-sum contest between self and others for control, and the integrity of actions is determined by their value-based authenticity. In this enlightening book, a Stanford Graduate School of Business professor challenges those assumptions by examining how power exists in the relationships between persons and how leaders should embrace different roles and performance, like actors, to strengthen those relationships and grow themselves.
Gary Hamel and Michele Zanini,Humanocracy: Creating Organizations as Amazing as the People Inside Them(Harvard Business Review Press)
The renowned business thinker and a management consultant describe how to create organizations that are more fully human—guided by a spirit of entrepreneurship, a shared sense of ownership, and a commitment to being an ongoing game-changer in the marketplace and society. Using both compelling calls to re-make the future of work and concrete examples of effective transformations, they show how leaders can overcome resistance to changing bureaucratic beliefs and structures.
Reed Hastings and Erin Meyer,No Rules Rules: Netflix and the Culture of Reinvention(Penguin)
The co-founder and CEO of Netflix and an INSEAD professor and author of the groundbreakingThe Culture Map(2014) explain how the company has defied traditional management practices to build a culture that prioritizes people, candor, context, and individual freedom and responsibility. The result is an organization able to adapt and reinvent itself amidst extraordinary changes in the worlds of technology and entertainment – while building a beloved brand and exceptional market value.
Rebecca Henderson,Reimagining Capitalism in a World on Fire(PublicAffairs)
In a far-reaching critique of contemporary Capitalism, a Harvard University professor offers a series of coordinated research-driven recommendations and alternatives that would re-make the firm, the world of finance, and the commitment to cooperate in building a better world. Like Mariana Mazzucato’sThe Value of Everything(2018) and Branko Milanovic’sCapitalism, Alone(2019), Henderson argues for a more systemic approach that more fully connects the market economy to a range of pressing environmental, social justice, and political realities.
Rosabeth Moss Kanter,Think Outside the Building: How Advanced Leaders Can Change the World One Smart Innovation at a Time(PublicAffairs)
Based in a decade’s research and a host of diverse examples, the legendary Harvard Business School management and innovation expert outlines how ‘advanced leadership’ can effect positive change across business, government, and community sectors. The skills and sensibilities she describes – from seeing the bigger picture and embracing causes to taking first steps, telling the right stories, and crafting coalitions – amount to a ‘call to lead’ meaningfully amidst today’s many challenging situations and wicked problems.
